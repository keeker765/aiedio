
## 1. Competitors Analysis

### Competitor 1: InVideo AI
*   **Product & Features:** A semi-automated platform that turns text prompts into stock-footage-based videos with AI voiceovers.
*   **Official Introduction:** InVideo AI - Text to Video Generation [<sup>1</sup>](https://invideo.io/ai/)
*   **Market Position:** Focused on "quick and easy" content for YouTube and marketing.
*   **Similarities:** Both aim to lower the barrier to video entry and use LLMs for script generation.
*   **Differences:** InVideo relies heavily on stock libraries. Our pipeline focuses on **generative consistency**, allowing users to create unique scenes from scratch rather than just stitching existing stock clips together.

### Competitor 2: Runway (Gen-3 Alpha)
*   **Product & Features:** Top-tier video-to-video and text-to-video generation tool known for high visual fidelity and physics-based motion.
*   **Official Introduction:** RunwayML - Gen-3 Alpha Research & Features [<sup>2</sup>](https://runwayml.com/ai-tools/gen-3-alpha/)
*   **Market Position:** Aimed at professional creators, filmmakers, and VFX artists.
*   **Similarities:** High emphasis on generative AI and visual quality.
*   **Differences:** Runway provides individual clips but lacks a **narrative pipeline**. Users have to manually edit clips together externally. Our project bridges this gap by automating the "Story → Clip → Edit" workflow in one go.

## 2. User Interviews

### Interview Log 1: Content Creator (Target: "Ah Jie")
*   **Discussion:** The user mentioned that current AI tools feel like a "lottery." Sometimes the face looks great, but in the next shot, the character is a different person.
*   **Outcome:** He needs tools that prioritize **visual continuity**. He is less interested in "perfect" pixels and more interested in a character that looks the same across 5 different scenes.

### Interview Log 2: Small Business Owner (Target: "Li Jie")
*   **Discussion:** The user explained that professional filming is simply too expensive for low-margin handmade products. She tried AI before, but the backgrounds were too distracting or "hallucinated" weird shapes.
*   **Outcome:** She needs **controllability**. The tool must allow her to keep specific product details accurate while automating the "boring" parts like lighting and transitions.

### Interview Log 3: Marketing Manager (Target: "Mr. Wang")
*   **Discussion:** Consistency is the biggest hurdle. His brand has a specific "cool blue" aesthetic, and generic AI tools often output random bright colors.
*   **Outcome:** He needs a **template-based system** where brand guidelines (colors, lighting) can be "locked in" across the entire generation pipeline.

## 3. User Hypotheses (UX Personas)

### Persona 1: The Busy Content Creator – "Ah Jie"
*   **Background:** 28 years old, independent creator on TikTok/Bilibili.
*   **Goals:** Post 1-2 videos daily to maintain engagement; experiment with new visual styles without spending hours filming.
*   **Requirements:** Needs a way to maintain a coherent "story" where characters don't change randomly between clips.
*   **Pain Points:** Traditional filming is too slow; current AI lacks a personal "humorous" touch and feels too templated.

### Persona 2: The Small Business Owner – "Li Jie"
*   **Background:** 35 years old, runs a handmade accessory shop on Taobao with a 2-person team.
*   **Goals:** Create 5-10 product showcase videos per week to increase conversion rates.
*   **Requirements:** Simple, one-click solutions that don't require professional editing skills.
*   **Pain Points:** High cost of hiring photographers; earlier AI attempts resulted in incorrect product details or irrelevant backgrounds.

### Persona 3: The Marketing Expert – "Mr. Wang"
*   **Background:** 42 years old, marketing manager at a mid-sized EdTech company.
*   **Goals:** Rapidly iterate multiple versions of video ads for A/B testing on social media.
*   **Requirements:** A tool that integrates with marketing workflows and supports quick modifications while maintaining brand consistency.
*   **Pain Points:** Internal approval is too slow; external agencies are too expensive; hard to iterate based on daily campaign data.

## 4. Value Proposition

Our solution stands out by shifting the focus from "generating a clip" to "**automating a story.**"

*   **Coherence Over Chaos:** Unlike random clip generators, our pipeline uses LLM-driven storyboards to ensure logical flow.
*   **Visual Stability:** We solve the "drifting character" problem by enforcing fixed seeds and prompt templates, which current competitors often leave to the user to figure out manually.
*   **End-to-End Efficiency:** We provide a single workflow that covers script, video, and audio. This removes the need for users to learn complex software like Premiere Pro or After Effects.

## 5. User Stories

These stories transform our user needs into actionable software requirements.

*   **Style Lock-in:** As a **Marketing Expert**, I want to define a fixed color palette and lighting style for my project, so that all generated clips look like they belong to the same brand campaign.
*   **Narrative Flow:** As a **Content Creator**, I want the system to generate a 4-scene storyboard based on my 1-sentence prompt, so that I have a coherent narrative structure before generating any video.
*   **Character Continuity:** As a **Content Creator**, I want to use a "reference character description" across all scenes, so that the lead character's appearance remains consistent throughout the short film.
*   **One-Click Composition:** As a **Small Business Owner**, I want the pipeline to automatically add transitions and background music, so that I can download a finished video ready for posting without manual editing.
*   **Efficiency:** As a **Small Business Owner**, I want to generate a 15-second product video from a text description in under 5 minutes, so that I can scale my content production without increasing my budget.
