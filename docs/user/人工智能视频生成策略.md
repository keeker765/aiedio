# AI Video Generation Strategy & UX Document

## 1. Competitors Analysis

### Competitor 1: InVideo AI
*   **Product & Features:** A semi-automated platform that turns text prompts into stock-footage-based videos with AI voiceovers.
*   **Official Introduction:** InVideo AI - Text to Video Generation
*   **Market Position:** Focused on "quick and easy" content for YouTube and marketing.
*   **Similarities:** Both aim to lower the barrier to video entry and use LLMs for script generation.
*   **Differences:** InVideo relies heavily on stock libraries. Our pipeline focuses on **generative consistency**, allowing users to create unique scenes from scratch rather than just stitching existing stock clips together.

### Competitor 2: Runway (Gen-3 Alpha)
*   **Product & Features:** Top-tier video-to-video and text-to-video generation tool known for high visual fidelity and physics-based motion.
*   **Official Introduction:** RunwayML - Gen-3 Alpha Research & Features
*   **Market Position:** Aimed at professional creators, filmmakers, and VFX artists.
*   **Similarities:** High emphasis on generative AI and visual quality.
*   **Differences:** Runway provides individual clips but lacks a **narrative pipeline**. Users have to manually edit clips together externally. Our project bridges this gap by automating the "Story → Clip → Edit" workflow in one go.

## 2. User Interview Transcripts & Findings

In order to gather actionable insights before building features and hypotheses, we designed the following semi-structured interview script:
1. **Current Workflow:** "Walk us through your current video production process. How long does it take from idea to post?"
2. **Pain Points:** "What is the single most expensive or time-consuming part of your process?"
3. **AI Experience:** "Have you used Gen-AI video tools before? If so, what was the 'deal-breaker' that made you stop or hesitate?"
4. **Desired Outcome:** "If you had a magic wand, what one repetitive task would you automate today?"

### Transcript 1: The Content Creator (Target: "Ah Jie")
*   **Q: Walk us through your current video production process. How long does it take from idea to post?**
    *   **A (Ah Jie):** "I usually spend Monday writing scripts, Tuesday filming myself in my living room with three different outfits, and Wednesday dragging it all into Premiere. It typically takes 3 to 4 days for a solid 1-minute video."
*   **Q: What is the single most expensive or time-consuming part of your process?**
    *   **A (Ah Jie):** "Definitely the actual filming and lighting setup. If I make a mistake, doing reshoots ruins the whole day."
*   **Q: Have you used Gen-AI video tools before? If so, what was the 'deal-breaker'?**
    *   **A (Ah Jie):** "I tried a few text-to-video platforms. The deal-breaker was the 'lottery effect'. In the first 5-second clip, my AI character had a beard, and in the next clip, he looked like a completely different teenager. I can't tell a story if the actor keeps mutating."
*   **Q: If you had a magic wand, what one repetitive task would you automate today?**
    *   **A (Ah Jie):** "I want to upload one photo of myself, write a script, and have the AI act out the whole scene consistently without me having to set up a real camera."

### Transcript 2: The Small Business Owner (Target: "Li Jie")
*   **Q: Walk us through your current video production process. How long does it take from idea to post?**
    *   **A (Li Jie):** "I clear my workbench, put my handmade jewelry on a white cloth, and shoot it with my iPhone. I use CapCut to add some text. From making the item to posting, it takes half a day. But the videos look very amateur."
*   **Q: What is the single most expensive or time-consuming part of your process?**
    *   **A (Li Jie):** "Hiring a professional lifestyle photographer or renting a studio. It eats my entire profit margin."
*   **Q: Have you used Gen-AI video tools before? If so, what was the 'deal-breaker'?**
    *   **A (Li Jie):** "Yes! I tried animating an image of my necklace. But the AI hallucinated extra beads or warped the shape of the pendant. When customers receive the real item, they complain it doesn't look like the video. The AI ruined my product's physical accuracy."
*   **Q: If you had a magic wand, what one repetitive task would you automate today?**
    *   **A (Li Jie):** "I want to keep my product image 100% untouched and physically accurate, but automatically generate a beautiful, moving background—like ocean waves or cinematic lighting sweeps."

### Transcript 3: The Marketing Manager (Target: "Mr. Wang")
*   **Q: Walk us through your current video production process. How long does it take from idea to post?**
    *   **A (Mr. Wang):** "We look at Monday’s ad CTR data. If it's low, I brief our external agency. They take 3 to 5 days to send back new video variants. By the time it's approved by legal, it’s the next week."
*   **Q: What is the single most expensive or time-consuming part of your process?**
    *   **A (Mr. Wang):** "Iteration speed and brand compliance. Agencies are too slow, and doing it in-house takes too much managerial back-and-forth."
*   **Q: Have you used Gen-AI video tools before? If so, what was the 'deal-breaker'?**
    *   **A (Mr. Wang):** "We experimented with Midjourney and Runway. The deal-breaker was the lack of enterprise controls. The AI ignores our strict corporate hex colors and messes up our logo placements. Our brand team rejected 90% of the AI outputs for non-compliance."
*   **Q: If you had a magic wand, what one repetitive task would you automate today?**
    *   **A (Mr. Wang):** "I'd lock in our brand templates—exact colors, typography, lighting styles—so that I could type 'new ad variant' and instantly get a compliant video without waiting 5 days."

## 3. Detailed UX Personas

By synthesizing interview feedback and utilizing standardized Persona definition methodologies, we have expanded and established three core user personas. We included summarizing quotes, demographics, and accessibility needs.

### Persona 1: The Efficiency-Driven Storyteller — Ah Jie
*   **Quote:** *"My homepage traffic is calculated daily; if I don't update, I'll be forgotten. But current AI generation is like a blind box—I can't tell a coherent story."*
*   **Demographics:** Real name: Zhang Jie / 28 / Male / Tier-1 City (Guangzhou) / Independent Full-time Blogger / BA in New Media.
*   **Bio:** Ah Jie is a "digital native" who survives on trend cycles. He needs to produce high-volume content, but the physical limits of filming, changing outfits, and setting up lighting every day bottleneck his studio.
*   **Tech Literacy:** High. Deeply understands internet memes, familiar with Midjourney prompting concepts, and uses CapCut/Premiere.
*   **Accessibility Needs:** Intensive late-night editing causes visual fatigue. Requires native **Dark Mode**. Reviews videos in noisy public spaces, needing strong visual cues for **auto-subtitles**.
*   **Frustrations:**
    *   **The "Lottery" Effect:** Character features (hair, face, clothes) randomly shift between clips.
    *   **Lack of Soul:** AI videos often pursue an overly "perfect texture," losing the humorous, relatable human touch.
*   **Key Requirement:** **Seed-based consistency**. The ability to upload reference photos and have the AI "act" as that same person across scenes.

### Persona 2: The Pragmatic Entrepreneur — Li Jie
*   **Quote:** *"Video is just a sales tool. Every dollar spent on a photographer is a dollar off my margin. But if the AI hallucinates my product shapes, I'll face mass returns!"*
*   **Demographics:** Li Li / 35 / Female / E-commerce Hub (Yiwu) / Married with a child / Handmade Accessory Shop Owner.
*   **Bio:** Li Jie runs a niche shop with thin margins (lean operation). To her, video is solely a conversion tool, not art. Hiring photographers is too expensive and ruins profitability.
*   **Tech Literacy:** Moderate. Proficient in mobile apps and e-commerce tools, but intimidated by complex English text prompts and desktop software.
*   **Accessibility Needs:** Often operates phones **one-handed/blindly** while packing goods. UI controls must follow **"thumb-zone layout"** with large, high-contrast buttons to prevent cognitive overload.
*   **Frustrations:**
    *   **Visual Hallucinations:** AI generating extra fingers or distorting the physical shape of real jewelry.
    *   **Distracting Backgrounds:** Fantasy-like backgrounds steal focus from the actual item.
*   **Key Requirement:** **Product-Centric Control**. Features like "background-swap/movement" keeping the item 100% physically realistic while animating only the environment.

### Persona 3: The Data-Driven Strategist — Mr. Wang
*   **Quote:** *"If Monday's ad CTR data drops, I need updated creatives live by Tuesday! Traditional ad agencies simply can't outrun this pace."*
*   **Demographics:** Wang Qiang / 42 / Male / Beijing / Married / Marketing Director at EdTech Corp.
*   **Bio:** Manages a multi-million-dollar ad budget with heavy ROI expectations. Videos are "variable assets" to be A/B tested continuously based on CTR.
*   **Tech Literacy:** Strategic. Cares about workflow integration and macros, never tweaks individual settings manually. Rejects/Approves based on dashboards.
*   **Accessibility Needs:** **Mild color blindness (Red-Green)**. Approval dashboards cannot rely solely on simple red/green color blocks; they require highly distinct icons (check/cross) and bold text. Needs **global shortcut keys** for batch approvals.
*   **Frustrations:**
    *   **Workflow Friction:** Unpredictable AI outputs frequently violate strict corporate brand guidelines and get blocked by legal.
    *   **Slow Iteration:** Agencies take weeks to tweak creatives based on data; trends die before fixing.
*   **Key Requirement:** **Enterprise Templates**. Hard-code "Brand Assets" (locking Hex colors, fixed watermark UI, forced aesthetics)—meaning junior staff cannot generate non-compliant visuals.

## 4. Value Proposition

Our solution stands out by shifting the focus from "generating a clip" to "**automating a story.**"

*   **Coherence Over Chaos:** Unlike random clip generators, our pipeline uses LLM-driven storyboards to ensure logical flow.
*   **Visual Stability:** We solve the "drifting character" problem by enforcing fixed seeds and prompt templates, which current competitors often leave to the user to figure out manually.
*   **End-to-End Efficiency:** We provide a single workflow that covers script, video, and audio. This removes the need for users to learn complex software like Premiere Pro or After Effects.

## 5. User Stories

These stories transform our user needs into actionable software requirements.

*   **Style Lock-in:** As a **Marketing Expert**, I want to define a fixed color palette and lighting style for my project, so that all generated clips look like they belong to the same brand campaign.
*   **Narrative Flow:** As a **Content Creator**, I want the system to generate a 4-scene storyboard based on my 1-sentence prompt, so that I have a coherent narrative structure before generating any video.
*   **Character Continuity:** As a **Content Creator**, I want to use a "reference character description" across all scenes, so that the lead character's appearance remains consistent throughout the short film.
*   **One-Click Composition:** As a **Small Business Owner**, I want the pipeline to automatically add transitions and background music, so that I can download a finished video ready for posting without manual editing.
*   **Efficiency:** As a **Small Business Owner**, I want to generate a 15-second product video from a text description in under 5 minutes, so that I can scale my content production without increasing my budget.
